A complication of diabetes caused by high blood sugar levels damaging the retina. It can lead to blindness if untreated.

Cataract

A clouding of the normally clear lens of the eye, similar to looking through a frosty or fogged-up window.

Glaucoma

A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often caused by abnormally high pressure in your eye.

Diabetic Retinopathy

This condition occurs in people with diabetes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye (retina). Initially, it might cause no symptoms or only mild vision problems. Eventually, it can cause blindness. Key signs our AI looks for include microaneurysms (small swellings in blood vessels), hemorrhages (bleeding), and hard exudates (lipid deposits).

×

Cataract

A cataract is a dense, cloudy area that forms in the lens of the eye. It begins when proteins in the eye form clumps that prevent the lens from sending clear images to the retina. The retina works by converting the light that comes through the lens into signals. It sends the signals to the optic nerve, which carries them to the brain. A cataract can make it difficult to read, drive a car (especially at night), or see the expression on a friend's face. The AI identifies this by detecting the characteristic opaqueness covering the lens.
